Data

1 genome

3 billion char sequence, ~700 megabytes

right off the genome sequencer, ~250 gigabytes

1 per 1000 base is variable in human population

all humans are 99.5% similar to any other humans

Variant calling

99.5% of genome data is shared across the genomes

By using the variant call format only the variations need to be stored along with a reference genome.

In VCF format a genome takes up ~125 megabytes

1000genomes.org

launched in January 2008, was an international research effort to establish by far the most detailed catalogue of human genetic variations including SNPs, structural variants, and their haplotype context. The final data set contains data for 2,504 individuals from 26 populations.

Map

The 100,000 Genomes Project

The project will sequence 100,000 genomes from around 70,000 people. Participants are patients with a rare disease, plus their families, and patients with cancer.
